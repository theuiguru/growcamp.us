About G.R.O.W.

The objective of G.R.O.W. Ministries is to help women grow in Christ throughout all the stages of their life and have a personal, life-changing relationship with God. This ministry is essential in that it provides a support system for young women while also educating them in the Orthodox faith.

The goal of the ministry is to further reach out to women by hosting events in different regions throughout the Northeast Diocese. The long term goal is to develop a fellowship among young women, encourage each other spiritually, and hold each other accountable.

Thoughts from Kochamma:

I am an activist and a visionary rooting my convictions and commitments in the Triune God. My firm faith that is translated into action derives its motivation and inspiration from the contextual theological reflections from the contexts in which the marginalised communities including my experience. The God who stands with the underside in History, who is witnessed to by generations of those that have tasted and experienced God’s hand in partnership with people who thirst for righteousness, justice and peace constantly and continuously sustains me. Therefore I have been able to demonstrate in and through my work that it is possible though difficult to bring about a positive change through strategic planning, creative -thinking outside the framework and parameters of accepted norms when required.

My Commitment is to a vision that believes in the possibility of building a new world with justice, peace and harmony. This compels me to be engaged in acts of liberation and transformation. I believe that I have accomplished this at different levels in different places with an emphasis on intellectual enterprises, Education, passionate Mission and Diakonia that enables to live out the Gospel and communitarian team work.

My ability to relate well with others and embrace diversity has been expressed through my own life. I have a good experience of working with many Christian faith denominations and religious groups through my work with CWM and beyond. I believe in the strength of Ecumenical Solidarity for the true witness of the Christian faith especially in this age of multi-plural contexts.

Camp Rules & Regulations

    Any individual that fails to comply with these regulations is subject to dismissal from the campus and sent home at his/her own expense. There will be no refunds and a written and verbal notification to the individual’s parish Vicar and Asst. Vicar shall be issued. There shall be no exceptions to these rules. Rules and regulations of GROW include, but are not limited to, the following:
  1. Attendance and participation in all meetings, general sessions, events, and activities are mandatory, with an emphasis on being punctual. Participants are not allowed to socialize or sleep during any sessions that require their attendance. Consequences for such behavior are removal from the Conference Center, phone call to parents and vicar.
  2. During the dates of the camp, June 17th– June 19th, 2016, the registrants are not allowed to leave the campus grounds for any reason, unless allowed by GROW Committee and officiating clergy.
  3. In the case of medical emergency, the GROW committee, officiating clergy, and the police of The Conference Center of Valley Forge should be informed immediately so that appropriate care can be obtained.
  4. All facility and sleeping rooms must be left in the condition found. No furniture may be moved out of the sleeping rooms or from lounge areas into individual rooms. Theft or vandalism of the Conference Center’s property is strictly prohibited
  5. There shall be no inappropriate physical or verbal harassment against the camp attendees, the GROW committee, or Valley Forge Conference Center employees.
  6. Smoking is prohibited in all buildings.
  7. There shall be no use, consumption, or possession of alcohol, drugs, hazardous/flammable materials, or weapons anywhere on the campus.
  8. The Conference Center at Valley Forge, GROW Ministries and the Northeast American Diocese of the Indian Orthodox Church are not responsible for any personal property that may be lost, stolen, or damaged.
  9. Unregistered visitors are prohibited from the buildings of The Conference Center of Valley Forge.
  10. Reasonable quiet is expected at all times. Please be considerate of others when entering and leaving the residence halls and other buildings.
  11. Cell phones are to be turned off or put on vibrate at all times.
  12. Officiating clergy, and GROW Committee members may dismiss any participant for reasons that violate the moral and ethical rules, standards and traditions of the Indian Orthodox Church.
